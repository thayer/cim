Molecular & structural imaging & sensing

Dartmouth faculty have led the development of a range of technologies at the interface between light and tissue. At this interface, different imaging tools elucidate fundamentally new information about the molecular concentrations or the sub-microscopic structures making up the tissue. In all cases, these signals must be interpreted and the systems designed by Monte Carlo modeling of the light-tissue interactions. Innovations in source illumination, detector design and image/spectral processing have led to devices which measure molecular probe concentrations, radiation dose delivery and sub-cellular scattering features of tissues.
